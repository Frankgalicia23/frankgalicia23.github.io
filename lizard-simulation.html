Understanding Natural Selection

Directional Selection

Favors one extreme phenotype over others, causing the population mean to shift. Often triggered by environmental changes like temperature shifts or droughts.

Stabilizing Selection

Favors intermediate phenotypes and selects against extreme variations. This narrows the bell curve around the average.

Diversifying (Disruptive) Selection

Favors both extremes while selecting against the intermediate phenotype. This can lead to a bimodal distribution (two peaks).
