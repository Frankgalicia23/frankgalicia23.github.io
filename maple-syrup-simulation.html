Matter Cycling & Photosynthesis (MS-LS1-6)

Where does the matter come from?

Sugar Maples don't get their "bulk" from the soil. Instead, they take in Carbon Dioxide (CO2) from the air and use Sunlight to turn it into Sugar (Glucose). This process is called photosynthesis.

Wood & Syrup (Matter Transformation)

The sugar created in the summer is used in two ways: 1) To build cellulose (the wood of the tree) and 2) Stored as starch which turns back into sugar in the sap during the New England spring thaw.

Environmental Impact

Changes in the environment, such as increased CO2 or Temperature, directly affect how much matter a tree can produce. By running multiple trials, we can observe how different conditions impact long-term forest health and syrup yields.
