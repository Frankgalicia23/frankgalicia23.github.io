Matter Cycling & Photosynthesis (MS-LS1-6)

Where does the matter come from?

Sugar Maples don't get their "bulk" from the soil. Instead, they take in Carbon Dioxide (CO2) from the air and use Sunlight to turn it into Sugar (Glucose). This process is called photosynthesis.

Wood & Syrup (Matter Transformation)

The sugar created through photosynthesis is used in two ways: 1) To build cellulose (the wood of the tree) and 2) Stored as starch which turns back into sugar in the sap during the New England spring thaw.

Heredity & Selection (MS-LS3/4)

Not all maple trees are the same! Some have genes for higher sugar content, while others grow faster wood. If the environment changes (like a warming climate), some trees may survive better than others, leading to evolution in the grove over generations.
